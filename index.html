How does COVID 19 spread?

The COVID 19 virus can spread from an infected person’s mouth or nose in small liquid particles when they cough, sneeze, speak, sing, or breathe. A person can be infected when droplets containing the COVID 19 virus are inhaled or come into direct contact with the eyes, nose, or mouth. Additionally, a person can get infected through touching surfaces that have been contaminated by the virus when touching their eyes, nose, or mouth without cleaning their hands. Evidence shows that the COVID 19 virus spreads mainly between people who have close contact with each other. To best avoid getting COVID 19 avoid the three Cs: Crowded places, Close contact settings, Confined and enclosed spaces.

Development of COVID 19 vaccines

Researchers have worked hard to develop vaccines. The speed of development of the vaccine is a result of collaborations between scientists, manufacturers, and distributors. Unlike other situations, planning phases have been run side by side with the vaccine developments, instead of running one after the other. The Therapeutic Goods Administration (TGA) assesses the vaccines before they are approved in Australia through multiple phases of testing. The vaccines are assessed on Safety, Quality and Effectiveness. Each batch of the vaccine is quality checked before distributing, making sure each batch meets the same quality requirements. Thanks to the community maintaining safe behaviors, like good hand hygiene and social distancing, there has been more time for testing of the vaccines
